Communication plan

How the Terrascope tutorial video series is designed, distributed, and measured. KU Leuven Faculty of Science, Science Communication and Outreach course.

Jorge Martínez Vázquez · Furkan Demir · Ayan Keykavoosi · Ahmed Taleb Ahmed Al-Qathmi

April 26, 2026

1. Objective

Introduce new users of Terrascope to the platform and smooth the learning curve so they can use its full functionality with confidence.

1.1 Goals

  • Reduce drop-off after sign-up by answering the most common questions of new users:
    1. What can I do here?
    2. Which tool do I start with?
    3. How do I complete my specific task?
  • Lower the perceived complexity of the platform for non-technical users.
  • Build confidence through short, actionable, visually consistent videos that can be watched in any order but work best as a sequence.

2. Target audience

  • Who: Master and PhD students in environmental science, geography, agriculture, urban planning, and adjacent fields.
  • Technical level: mixed. Some have coding experience, others only want ready-to-use products.
  • Pain points: limited time, weak documentation, no powerful local computer.
  • Preferred format: short videos (2 to 4 minutes) with screen recordings for concrete examples and a visible narrator. Minimal jargon.

3. Key messages

  • Terrascope is a free, powerful, cloud-based platform that works without a powerful computer.
  • It can be used with little or no coding knowledge, and scales up to OpenEO and Jupyter for complex tasks.
  • Each video introduces its topic and ends by suggesting the next logical video.

5. Video design framework

Every video follows a common template so the series feels coherent.

5.1 Visual and audio style

  • Intro (a few seconds): satellite image montage fading into the Terrascope logo.
  • Host: on-screen narrator at the start to establish a personal connection and explain the topic.
  • Screen recording: high contrast, zoom in or red-circle the relevant buttons, yellow highlights for important text.
  • Duration: 2 to 3 minutes per video.
  • End: next video title, link to related documentation, link to the page where the demo took place.

5.2 Narrative structure

  1. What problem does this video solve, and what tool does it explain?
  2. Step-by-step demo in 2 to 3 minutes. Avoid too many steps. Label them and keep the step title in the upper corner.
  3. Recap: repeat the steps verbally and show them on screen as bullet points.
  4. Bridge to the next video (10 to 15 seconds): suggest the next video in the intended order.

5.3 Language and tone

Use "you" and "we". Keep an informal tone rather than academic. Avoid jargon. Always explain acronyms on first use. Use concrete examples close to the userbase (Belgium as the default location). Encourage the viewer to apply the concept right after instruction so they can follow along.

5.4 Metadata and accessibility

  • Title format: "Terrascope tutorial #: short title". Example: "Terrascope tutorial 1: Welcome to Terrascope, Viewer, EOplaza and Cloud processing".
  • Description: short description box with links to the pages used and to relevant documentation.
  • Captions: English subtitles (and other languages where possible). A transcript is also valuable.

6. Promotion and distribution

  • Terrascope website: add a "Video tutorials" link in the top bar that opens a playlist (currently behind "User support", but it should be immediately visible).
  • Documentation portal: link every video to its relevant documentation page and vice versa.
  • Onboarding email: after sign-up, an automated email encourages viewers to start with Video 1. A one-time email goes to existing users.
  • Forum: post each video as a topic. Pin the welcome video. Encourage questions and requests for future video topics.
  • University channels: ask KU Leuven professors teaching subjects where Terrascope is useful to share the page with students.

6.1 Measuring success

  • Video views in the first three months.
  • Average watch time (percentage of video length watched), to identify content that frustrates viewers and causes drop-off.
  • Forum posts, questions, and suggestions referencing the series.
